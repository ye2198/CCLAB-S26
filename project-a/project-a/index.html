Parasite

Scientific Name:

Monozygotic Ascaris

Discovered by / at:

Discovered while detecting symptoms of disease and weakening inside other organisms.

Symptoms: Immune system weakening, memory loss, loss of control over bodily functions.

Extraction impossible while keeping subject alive.

Family:

Inside the broad family of Ascaris.

Habitat:

Inside other larger living organisms, typically found in larger mammals. Most effective and harmful inside humans.

Appearance:

Worm-like appearance, color ranging from red to orange-pink, pulsating, bulging eyes, unpredictable movement.

Key Characteristics:

  • Unpredictable, frantic movements: doesn't seem to be aware of its surroundings.
  • Eyes directed towards general direction the parasite is moving towards: vision may be limited.
  • Grows if shown direction food is located in, shrinks and fades if starved until its death.
  • Body pulsates and wiggles to move.

What we know so far about Parasite? So far， we know this creature has rapid, unpredictable movements as it wants to consume more organisms as fast as possible to ensure its own survival. It can only survive within living organisms, as they contain the nutrients necessary to the parasite's survival. Its intellectual potential is, however, quite low, and seems to have trouble finding nutrients on its own: during research, our scientists actively provided support to maintain the parasite in a living state.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature We can interact with this creature by clicking anywhere on the screen, which will lead it to follow the position of the mouse: we are essentially showing it where to look for food to consume. Once left alone, it’ll continue doing what it does best; wandering in search of more nutrients. However, if left alone for too long, it'll gradually disappear until death: make sure to keep clicking to see it grow.
